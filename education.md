        <p>My time at the University of Oregon wasn't just about earning my degree-it was about learning how to be successful in one aspect of your life when others are showing difficulties. Juggling struggles in my personal life, struggles with my mental health and learning to be an adult while trying to find something I was passionate about specializing in was at times, too much to handle. But, I never stopped trying to find a solution. While I was hit with many speed bumps I continued to push forward. I learned how to take accountability for my work, remain adaptable in the face of setbacks and leverage proactive problem-solving when navigating complex challenges. While I am thankful for the amazing technical skills I learned in class, these professional attributes are what I am proud to have cultivated.
        </p>

        <p>My academic journey at the University of Oregon allowed me to explore the intersection of logic and creativity. While my Computer Science major provided a rigorous technical foundation, my minors in Multimedia and Dance taught me the importance of visual communication.</p>

        <p>In Multimedia I took classes that forced me to understand different users' perspectives. I learned how to use the Adobe suite as well as a program called Processing. Processing is essentially a wrapper on java that allows artists to code different projects in a way that makes more sense to the artist's brain. Interacting with this pushed my coding skills and artistic skills together to create different mathematically engaging art pieces. It's so interesting that people thought about how many artists could benefit from an “easier” way to code. The classes I took for this minor gave me a deep understanding about how people approach software and programs from different views. How can my work affect someone's experience with a piece of software?
        </p>

        <p>I have been studying dance since I was in elementary school. I trained pre-professionally around 30+ hours a week for middle and high school. So there was no question about continuing my studies in college. The dance department at the University of Oregon taught me how to work with people from different backgrounds. Whether that is dance background or socio-economic background, I know how to facilitate group dynamics that include and highlight each member while producing good work. Because dance is such a personal art choreographing in a group can be very tricky. This taught me how to effectively communicate in group project settings without creating conflicts. 
        </p>

        <p>During the Winter 2026 term I took a software methodology class where we were instructed to build a functioning project in 8 weeks. I worked in a team of four where we built a scheduler website for the university's Multi Cultural Center(MCC). The MCC overviews more than 30 campus clubs and their weekly activities. Our scheduler allows the user to select specific clubs or all clubs to view their events as well as get an ICS link to import the events into their calendar. It also allows upper admin (MCC staff) and club admins (club board members) to edit the event and collaborate with other clubs on events. I worked on the front end of this project allowing my multimedia classes to blend into my computer science ones. The team work skills I learned through dance also benefitted when we sifted through all our ideas and plans for the scheduler. 
        </p>
